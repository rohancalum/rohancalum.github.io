Rohan Nuttall

I am a graduate student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Alberta. I'm interested in how reinforcement learning, explainable artificial intelligence and computational sustainability can help us build better societies. I'm also interested in the economics of inequality, roboethics and mitigating climate change impacts.

Previously, I interned with Kindred Systems and was a research assistant with the Energy, Technology and Architecture Lab at the University of British Columbia (UBC). There, I worked on machine learning models to understand the impacts of future climate change on the built environment. I also launched a sustainability open data portal for the university.

Before this, I enjoyed exploring the world of physics. I had the priviledge of working at TRIUMF on radioisotope production, as well as experimental particle physics with the PIENU collaboration.
