Rohan Nuttall

I am a graduate student with the Reinforcement and Artificial Intelligent Lab at the University of Alberta. My research focusses on how planning, causality, and model-based learning can improve the success of real-world applications of reinforcement learning. I'm interested in how these techniques, as well as eplainable artificial intelligence and computational sustainability can help us build better societies. I'm also interested in the economics of inequality, roboethics, mitigating climate change impacts, and how technology, data and strategy can help improve biodiversity, conservation and livelihoods.

Previously, I interned with Kindred Systems and was a research assistant with the Energy, Technology and Architecture Lab at the University of British Columbia (UBC). There, I worked on machine learning models to understand the impacts of future climate change on the built environment. I also launched a sustainability open data portal for the university.

Before this, I enjoyed exploring the world of physics. I learned about the complexities of producing radioisotopes at TRIUMF, as well as how elegant particle physics experiments are designed with the PIENU collaboration.
